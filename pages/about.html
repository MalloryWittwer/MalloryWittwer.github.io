About me

I am an independent web developer, materials scientist, and machine learning engineer based in Switzerland. I specialize in creating web-based demonstrators, data visualizations, and software tools that enable scientists to showcase their work effectively to the public and to the academic community. I love creating interactive applications that make it possible to understand technical concepts at a glance - because a web app is worth a thousand equations! I am also trained in data science and AI, and I am knowledgeable about climate tech, aerospace, and the industry 4.0. Finally, I am a strong advocate of open science. I am particularly interested in exploring how digital platforms can be leveraged to support the scientific community.

Background

In 2018, I graduated from EPFL in Materials Science and Engineering. I then learned data science and machine learning at EPFL's Extension School. From 2018 to 2021, I was a research associate under Prof. Matteo Seita at NTU in Singapore. I have coordinated projects investigating how machine learning can be applied to microscopy for high-throughput materials characterization. I published two papers in peer-reviewed scientific journals. I came back in Switzerland in August 2021 and, in December, I completed Le Wagon's Full-stack web development Bootcamp in 9 weeks.
